Changemaker Assessment

ABOUT

A changemaker is someone who actively seeks to effect positive and sustainable change within themselves, their local community, an organization, the wider society, or globally. This journey towards change is inherently a collective, creative endeavor, rooted in collaboration.

Roles:

The assessment covers a variety of changmaker roles, such as Advocates, Builders, Creators, Healers, Storytellers, etc. Each role represents a unique set of qualities and contributions towards societal dynamics and change.

Instructions:

  1. Read Carefully: Begin by reading each role's description to understand its significance and characteristics.
  2. Assess Honestly: For each statement, reflect on how well it resonates with your experiences, beliefs, and behaviors.
  3. 3. Choose Your Response: Select the response that best represents your level of agreement with each statement. Be as honest and spontaneous as possible.
  4. No Right or Wrong Answers: There are no correct or incorrect responses. This assessment is about your personal perception and self-reflection.
  5. Complete All Items: Ensure you respond to all items for each role for a comprehensive self-assessment.

Your Results:

Your results are presented below. As you read them, think about the following:

Explore Each Role Further: Visit our website at Inspire to Change - Changemaker Roles to delve deeper into each role and learn more about their significance in driving societal change.

Join Our Community: Share your results and connect with others on our online community at Inspire to Change Community. Engage in discussions, seek advice, and be part of a supportive network.

Social Media Sharing: We encourage you to share your assessment results on social media using the hashtag #MyChangemakerRole. This is a great way to connect with others who share similar interests and to spread awareness about the diverse roles in changemaking.

Reflect on Your Scores: Higher scores indicate a stronger natural alignment with that role. Reflect on what these alignments mean for you personally and professionally.

Areas for Growth: Consider lower scores as opportunities for personal development or exploration of new roles, or opportunities for collaboration.

Remember, this assessment is a tool for self-discovery and growth, offering insights into how you align with different roles in the changemaking spectrum. It's an opportunity to reflect, connect, and take the next steps in your journey as a changemaker.
